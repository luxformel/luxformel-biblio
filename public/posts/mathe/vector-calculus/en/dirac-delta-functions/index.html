Dirac Delta Function#

Exercise 1: Basic Delta Integrals#

Evaluate the following integrals:

  1. $\displaystyle \int_{2}^{6} (3x^2 - 2x - 1) , \delta(x - 3) , dx$
  2. $\displaystyle \int_{0}^{5} \cos x ; \delta(x - \pi) , dx$
  3. $\displaystyle \int_{-1}^{3} x^3 , \delta(x + 1) , dx$
  4. $\displaystyle \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \ln(x + 3) , \delta(x + 2) , dx$
  5. $\displaystyle \int_{0}^{\infty} e^{-x} , \delta(x - 2) , dx$

Exercise 2: Delta with Scaled & Shifted Arguments#

Evaluate the following integrals:

  1. $\displaystyle \int_{-2}^{2} (2x + 3) , \delta(3x) , dx$
  2. $\displaystyle \int_{0}^{2} (x^3 + 3x + 2) , \delta(1 - x) , dx$
  3. $\displaystyle \int_{-1}^{1} 9x^2 , \delta(3x + 1) , dx$
  4. $\displaystyle \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} f(x) , \delta(ax - b) , dx$ for $a \ne 0$
  5. $\displaystyle \int_{0}^{4} \sin(\pi x) , \delta!\left(x - \frac{1}{2}\right) dx$

Exercise 3: Properties & Relations#

  1. Prove: $\displaystyle x \frac{d\delta(x)}{dx} = -\delta(x)$
    Hint: Use integration by parts.
  2. Using $\theta(x)$ (Heaviside step function), show $\displaystyle \frac{d\theta}{dx} = \delta(x)$.
  3. Show: $\displaystyle \delta(ax) = \frac{1}{|a|}\delta(x)$ for $a \ne 0$.
  4. For a smooth function $f(x)$ with a single zero at $x_0$ in $(a,b)$, show:
    $\displaystyle \delta(f(x)) = \frac{\delta(x - x_0)}{|f’(x_0)|}$.

Exercise 4: Charge Densities#

  1. Write the volume charge density $\rho(\mathbf{r})$ for a point charge $q$ at $\mathbf{r}’$ such that $\int \rho , d\tau = q$.
  2. Write $\rho(\mathbf{r})$ for an electric dipole: $-q$ at origin, $+q$ at $\mathbf{a}$.
  3. Find $\rho(r)$ (spherical coordinates) for a uniform spherical shell of radius $R$ and total charge $Q$.
  4. Write $\rho(\mathbf{r})$ for a uniform infinite line charge along the $z$-axis with linear density $\lambda$.

Exercise 5: 3D Delta Integrals#

Evaluate:

  1. $\displaystyle \int_{\text{all space}} (r^2 + \mathbf{r} \cdot \mathbf{a} + a^2) , \delta^3(\mathbf{r} - \mathbf{a}) , d\tau$,
    where $\mathbf{a}$ fixed, $a = |\mathbf{a}|$.
  2. $\displaystyle \int_V |\mathbf{r} - \mathbf{b}|^2 , \delta^3(\mathbf{r}) , d\tau$,
    where $V$ is a cube of side 2 centered at origin, $\mathbf{b} = 4\hat{y} + 3\hat{z}$.
  3. $\displaystyle \int_V \bigl[r^4 + r^2(\mathbf{r} \cdot \mathbf{c}) + c^4\bigr] \delta^3(\mathbf{r} - \mathbf{c}) , d\tau$,
    where $V$ is sphere radius 6, $\mathbf{c} = 5\hat{x} + 3\hat{y} + 2\hat{z}$, $c = |\mathbf{c}|$.
  4. $\displaystyle \int_V (\mathbf{r} \cdot \mathbf{d}) , \delta^3(\mathbf{e} - \mathbf{r}) , d\tau$,
    where $\mathbf{d} = (1,2,3)$, $\mathbf{e} = (3,2,1)$, $V$ is sphere radius 1.5 centered at $(2,2,2)$.

Exercise 6: Delta in Divergence Context#

Evaluate

$$ J = \int_V e^{-r} \left( \nabla \cdot \frac{\hat{\mathbf{r}}}{r^2} \right) d\tau $$

where $V$ is sphere radius $R$ centered at origin, using:

  1. Direct delta-function identity for $\nabla \cdot (\hat{\mathbf{r}}/r^2)$.
  2. Divergence theorem + explicit computation.

Exercise 7: Advanced Delta Manipulations#

  1. Compute $\displaystyle \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} \delta’(x) f(x) , dx$ in terms of $f’(0)$.
  2. Verify: $\displaystyle \delta(x^2 - a^2) = \frac{1}{2|a|} \bigl[ \delta(x - a) + \delta(x + a) \bigr]$.
  3. Evaluate: $\displaystyle \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} \sin^2 x ; \delta’!(x) , dx$.
  4. Show: $\displaystyle \frac{d}{dx} \theta(x^2 - 1) = \delta(x - 1) - \delta(x + 1)$.
